1 """
2 Phase-fixed Pauli. In this representation, the FixedPhasePauli string operator is represented as two binary strings, one for x and one for z.
3 We do not keep track of the phase inside of this type
4
5 The format is as follows:
6
7 (i)^θ Z^z1 X^x1 ⊗ Z^z2 X^x2 ⊗ ⋯ ⊗ Z^zN X^xN
8
9 Products of operators simply concatonate the left and right strings separately. For example,
10
11 XYZIY = 11001|01101
12
13
14 To create a Y operator, bits in the same locations in `z` and `x` should be on.
15 This means that we have a phase to keep track of because Z^1*X^1 = iY.
16 As such, we end up working with operators of the form:
17
18 (i)^θ σ1 ⊗ σ2 ⊗ σ3 ⊗ ⋯ ⊗ σN,
19
20 where,
21
22 σ ∈ {X, iY, Z, I}
23
24 However, in this case the phase, θ, is fixed to ensure each Pauli string is Hermitian.
25 The phase angle can be obtained from the function `phase()`
26
27 Because the phase is fixed, a product of `FixedPhasePauli` types does not yield another `FixedPhasePauli` type.
28 This is because each pauli product creates a new phase. As such, the product of two `FixedPhasePauli`'s yields
29 a `Pauli` type.
30
31 See also [`Pauli`](@ref), [`ScaledPauli`](@ref).
32 """

64 i in ['I', 'Z', 'X', 'Y'] || error("Bad string: ", str)
65 end
